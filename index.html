About the work

Mechanical ideas, built visually.

My projects usually start with curiosity: how a system is arranged, how parts connect, how a model can be simplified, and how the final design can still feel believable. I like the full path from concept to final design — blocking things out, solving the messy parts, cleaning up the model, and presenting it in a way that feels intentional.

Process

From idea to finished design.

I like working through the whole design process instead of only focusing on the final image. The problem solving is usually the part I enjoy most.

01

Understand the idea

I start by looking at what the object or system is trying to communicate: the main shapes, the function, and the parts that need to feel important.

02

Block it out

I build the big forms first, test proportions, and organize the model before adding too much detail.

03

Solve and refine

This is where I adjust pipe routes, repeated parts, spacing, materials, scale, and clarity until the design starts to feel right.

04

Clean up the final

I optimize where I can, improve the lighting and composition, and use Photoshop when needed to make the final visual feel more finished.
